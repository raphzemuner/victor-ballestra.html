Without a will, you die "intestate" and your estate is divided according to strict legal rules — which may not reflect your wishes at all. Unmarried partners receive nothing, no matter how long you've been together. Specific gifts to friends or charities are ignored. Writing a will is the only way to make sure what you've built goes where you intend.
